01 — About

Research Profile

I am a 2nd year PhD student in Chemistry at Carnegie Mellon University , where I work on atomically precise gold nanoclusters with a focus on their exceptional optical and photonic properties — including NIR-to-visible photon upconversion and shortwave infrared emission.

I have passed the oral portion of my qualifying exam and have published in JACS, Angew. Chem. Int. Ed., Acc. Chem. Res., J. Phys. Chem. Lett., and Chem. Commun. I am now actively transitioning toward AI for Materials, particularly leveraging large language models and autonomous experimentation frameworks to accelerate materials discovery.

Beyond gold nanoclusters, my research background spans a range of functional material systems — including metal-organic frameworks (MOFs), metal-organic compounds, 3D printing materials, and organic photosensitizers — with hands-on project experience in each area.
